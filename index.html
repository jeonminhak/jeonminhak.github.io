About-me

I am a highly motivated Civil & Environmental Engineering student specializing in water resource management. My ultimate goal is to become a professor and researcher dedicated to solving the complex challenges of urban water systems through data-driven, intelligent solutions. I am particularly passionate about bridging the gap between traditional hydraulic engineering and modern computational science to create more resilient and sustainable water infrastructure for the future.

My hands-on experience as a project manager and engineer in water distribution system maintenance has provided me with a unique, ground-level perspective on these challenges. Working directly with field data from pipelines—analyzing pressure, flow, and quality to detect anomalies—I learned the critical importance of accurate, real-time analysis. This experience was the catalyst for my desire to pursue graduate-level research, as I realized the immense potential for AI and predictive models to move beyond reactive problem-solving.

To bridge this gap, I am currently self-studying Python and data analysis tools, including Pandas and the EPANET simulator, to build my computational skills. I believe that combining my practical field experience with advanced analytical capabilities will allow me to contribute a unique and valuable perspective to the academic world. I am eager to join a research lab where I can apply my passion and grow into a researcher who develops not just theoretically sound, but also practically applicable solutions.
